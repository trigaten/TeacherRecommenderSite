Inspiration

Hello, my name is Sander Schulhoff. I originally created this system in ~2019 when a college counselor at my highschool wanted to make the process of matching students with their teacher reccomenders for college more efficient.

The Problem
Highschool students need college recommendation letters from their teachers. Usually, they need 2 recommendation letters, one from a STEM teacher and one from a non-STEM teacher. Students go around asking teachers if they can letters for them. This can result in teachers overcommitting to writing too many letters or feeling pressured to say yes to the first students who ask them. Using this method makes it difficult to keep track of whether all students have confirmed recommenders.
The Solution
This system use Google Forms to collect both student and teacher preferences. It also allows teachers to set limits as to how many recommendation letters they will write. After the forms are filled out, a simple algorithm finds the best pairings and generates a Google Sheet with them. The simple Google Sheet interface used by college counselors allows them to keep track of the entire process and make sure all students are on track.
The System
College counselors control the system through a sidebar in a Google Sheet. It has clearly defined steps and allows you to send automatically generate forms, send emails (and reminder emails!), add/remove students and teachers, and edit any results before automatically sending them out.
